## Overview

The **Manage \<App\> X.Y.Z** entry you see in the app menu (where `\<App\>` is the
product/codename and `X.Y.Z` is the installed version, e.g. *Manage Rox 0.2.4*)
is surfaced by the **auto-updater**. It groups everything related to keeping the
desktop app up to date in one place. The version number is the build you are
currently running.

## Menu items

- **Check for Updates…** — Manually asks the update server whether a newer
  release exists. If one is found it begins downloading in the background; if you
  are already current, you'll get a "You're up to date" confirmation. The app
  also checks periodically on its own, so you rarely need this.

- **Release Channel** — Chooses which stream of builds you receive:
  - **Stable** — Production releases. Recommended for everyday use.
  - **Beta / Preview** — Earlier access to upcoming features; may be less stable.
  Switching channels changes which updates are offered the next time the app
  checks.

- **Restart to Update** — Appears once an update has finished downloading.
  Selecting it relaunches the app and applies the pending update. Until you
  restart, you keep running the current version.

- **About \<App\>** — Shows the exact version, build/commit, and license
  information. Useful when filing a bug report.

## How updates are delivered

Updates download in the background. The app never interrupts your work to install
one — it waits until you explicitly choose **Restart to Update** (or until the
next launch). This means an available update is safe to ignore until a convenient
moment.

<Callout type="info">
  The exact label depends on your build. The product name and version shown in
  the menu always match the running app, so "Manage Rox 0.2.4" and
  "Manage \<App\> X.Y.Z" refer to the same menu.
</Callout>
